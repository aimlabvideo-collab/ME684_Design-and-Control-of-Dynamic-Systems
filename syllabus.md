## Course Description

Prerequisite: ME 484. The course emphasizes design techniques for dynamic
control systems. Classical and modern control design methods are introduced as
alternative approaches to designing dynamic systems with controllers that
process sensor feedback to cause dynamic systems to meet tracking
specifications. Case studies examine the design and control of dynamic systems
in the real world.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

- Understand and construct mathematical models of dynamic systems.
- Analyze and design control systems.
- Develop state-space models and use them for system design and stability analysis.
- Design controllers (e.g., PID, state-feedback) to meet performance specifications.
- Strengthen problem-solving and critical thinking through case studies and a final design project.
